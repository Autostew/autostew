How are drivers rated?

Drivers have two independent ratings: the safety class and the performance rating.

First and most important is the safety class. It describes how safe a driver is. The performance rating describes how much a driver wins or loses against other drivers.

How is the driver safety class calculated?

See here a description of each safety class.

The Project CARS dedicated server reports impacts during the race, including the magnitude of them. Each time you have a collision, it's magnitude gets added to your safety rating, so a higher rating means a more unsafe driver. Collisions against the environment yield a maximum of 250 points, collisions against human drivers yield a maximum of 1.000 points. Collisions against AI drivers are not considered.

For each clean lap you complete, your safety rating will improve. Complete many clean laps, and you will get a good safety rating.

If a driver crashes excessively, she or he may be kicked from the game. That driver may rejoin immediately to the game if the event is still joinable (you will not be able to rejoin if the race is in progress). Eventually, an unsafe driver will reach the lowest safety class. Autostew will be strict with crashes by drivers in the lowest class until their behaviour improves.

Crashes among human drivers will be weighted according to their safety class. Therefore if a driver with a bad rating crashes with a driver with a good rating, the well-rated driver will be automatically considered "less at fault" and will be awarded less crash points. The worse rated driver will always be awarded the full points count. A crash between two drivers of the same class will award both the full crash points.

How is the driver performance rating calculated?

We use the Elo rating system. You will be considered a winner over every driver who finished in a worse position than yours, but also over all drivers who completed a lap in any stage of the race but did not finish the race itself.

Autostew logs crashes in a wierd way!

Autostew cannot distinguish who is at fault in a crash. Also, as you will be playing online, it may happen that in your screen no crash happened, but the dedicated server still logs a crash. Even with its quirks, the system should in the long term assign good ratings to safe drivers and bad ratings to unsafe drivers.
